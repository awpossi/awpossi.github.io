Bat Speed and Hitter Success in MLB: Full Interactive Report

This analysis explores the impact of average bat speed on offensive performance, identifies efficient hitters, and segments MLB players into swing-based clusters.

Executive Summary

This report analyzes how important average bat speed is for Major League Baseball hitters and whether it influences overall offensive success. We define success as a player's 'run value'-a metric that estimates how many runs a hitter contributes above average.

Using player swing data (e.g., contact quality, whiff rate, swing traits), we built two predictive models: a linear regression and a machine learning model (random forest). These models show that bat speed is an important contributor to offensive value, but it's not the only one.

We also clustered hitters into four types based on their swing characteristics to better understand which player types are most successful.

Methods & Data

We used a dataset of MLB hitters including average bat speed, whiff rate (swing and miss), swing length, and other metrics tied to contact and power. We defined success by 'batter run value', a metric summarizing how much a hitter contributes offensively.

Two models were built:
- Linear Regression: Evaluates how strongly bat speed and other traits predict success.
- Random Forest: A machine learning model that captures complex interactions between swing traits.

We also applied a clustering technique (KMeans) to group hitters into four archetypes based on their traits.

Key Findings

* Bat speed is positively associated with success. Faster swings generally result in higher run values.
* However, bat speed alone does not explain everything. Contact quality (how well a ball is hit), swing efficiency, and the ability to avoid swinging and missing also play big roles.
* The Random Forest model showed improved accuracy, meaning non-linear combinations of traits help explain performance better.
* Some players overperform their expected value ("efficient hitters"). Others have high potential due to strong swing traits but haven't yet reached peak value.

Clustering: Defining Hitter Archetypes

We used clustering to group hitters into four types based on swing data. This helps identify player types, such as:

1. High Bat Speed, High Whiff: Power hitters who swing hard but miss often.
2. Efficient Contact Hitters: Moderate speed but low whiff and consistent contact.
3. Balanced Hitters: Decent power, good swing efficiency, balanced approach.
4. Low Power, High Contact: Lower bat speed, more conservative swing profiles.

These clusters help with scouting, development, and forecasting player performance.

Conclusions & Recommendations

Bat speed is a key ingredient to offensive success but works best when paired with swing efficiency and contact ability.

Teams should consider:
- Not overvaluing bat speed alone-context matters.
- Using swing data to flag underperformers with high upside.
- Targeting players who outperform their tools (efficient hitters).
- Developing players based on their archetype.

Next steps could include integrating zone control, year-over-year forecasting, or predicting future run value using additional plate discipline data.
